# Research Notes

## Competitor Analysis

### Mint (Personal Finance)
- **Features**: Budget tracking, expense categorization, credit score monitoring, bill reminders, investment tracking
- **User Flow**: Connect bank accounts, automatic transaction import, set budgets, view spending trends
- **Gaps**: Limited goal setting, no habit tracking, focused on finance only

### YNAB (You Need A Budget)
- **Features**: Zero-based budgeting, goal setting for savings, debt payoff planning, envelope system
- **User Flow**: Import transactions, assign to categories, set goals, track progress
- **Gaps**: No investment tracking, limited habit building, no risk assessment

### Habitica (Habit Building)
- **Features**: Gamified habit tracking, task management, rewards system, social accountability
- **User Flow**: Create habits/tasks, complete daily, earn points, level up
- **Gaps**: No financial planning, limited long-term goal setting, no risk control

### Trello (Productivity/Planning)
- **Features**: Kanban boards, card-based task management, collaboration, due dates, checklists
- **User Flow**: Create boards for projects, add cards for tasks, move through lists
- **Gaps**: No financial calculations, no habit automation, not specifically for life planning

### Market Gaps
- Integrated life planning: combining financial, personal goals, habits, and risk assessment
- Family collaboration features
- Multi-platform with seamless sync
- AI-powered insights and recommendations
